Charles Danko

Assistant Professor

Charles is an assistant professor at the Baker Institute and the College of Veterinary Medicine, Cornell University. His research is focused on understanding gene regulation using methods from molecular biology and genetics, computer science, statistics, and machine learning. Learn about active research projects in the Research page.

@charlesdanko CV

Ed Rice

Laboratory Manager

Ed is a professional laboratory manager, with over 20 years of molecular biology experience. As the lead bench scientist, Ed has his hands in virtually every project in the Danko lab. He has a particular scientific interest in understanding the molecular pathways underlying autoimmune disorders.

Tinyi Chu

Croucher Foundation Graduate Fellow

Tinyi is a graduate student in the Computational Biology program at Cornell and a scholar of the Croucher Foundation. He is broadly interested in understanding how transcriptional regulatory networks determine cell fates during differentiation, using Treg and Th17 CD4+ T-cells as a model system.

Lauren Choate

GG&D Graduate Student

Lauren is a graduate student in the Genetics, Genomics, and Development program at Cornell. She is interested in understanding the role of disease risk alleles on transcriptional regulation in autoimmunity using computational and wet lab techniques.

Andre Martins

Contractor

Andre arrived at Cornell University with a strong background in computer science and software engineering, and has since become an expert in computational biology and gene regulation. He completed his Ph.D. in the Computational Biology program at Cornell in 2014, and is working as an independent contractor from the sunnier shores of Portugal. At his office in Lisbon, Andre continues to develop computational models that help unlock the secrets of gene regulation.

Hsiao-Pei Yang

Contractor

Hsiao-Pei Pei received her Ph.D. from Cornell University in 2001 in ecology and evolutionary biology under the guidance of Alexey Kondrashov. She is interested in taking theoretical, computational and empirical approaches to address questions in molecular population genomics. Currently, she is focusing on learning how slightly deleterious mutations in humans affect the adaptive immune system. Hsiao-Pei works with the Danko lab through a collaboration with the CBSU.

Zhong Wang

Programer, Postdoctroal Associate

Zhong Wang arrived at Cornell University with 6 years of experience in Statistical Genetics and Biostatistics. He conducted postdoctoral training at Penn State College of Medicine and Yale School of Public Health, where he worked in the areas of statistical modeling and software development. Before that, Zhong worked in the IT industry as a software engineer after he received his Ph.D. degree from Dalian University of Technology in China in 2000. Now he is focusing on developing software packages useful in the analysis of functional genomic data.
